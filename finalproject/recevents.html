Our Top 5

Georgia has a ton of places to see and things to do and we're here to help you and your family enjoy your stay here in Georgia; so here is our hand-picked list of the top five destinations Georgia has to offer!




1 / 5

#5: Ponce City Market, Atlanta, GA

A part of Atlanta's unique history and culture, Ponce City Market takes the number five spot on our list of places to see. Ponce City Market, off the iconic Ponce de Leon Avenue, is one of our favorite destinations here in Atlanta. In 2014, an old, abandoned Sears building was converted to the mixed-use center that is now known as Ponce City Market. You and your family could easily get lost, spending an entire day here considering the many things to explore at Ponce City Market. Starting with the restaurants and bars, Ponce City Market has some of the best restaurants and bars (as well as a winery) in the city of Atlanta with many different kinds of food, ranging from burgers, italian, ramen, sushi, and many more. On top of this, Ponce City Market also has quite a few stores inside that sell a ton of different things like vintage clothing, books, art, jewelry, and even a candy store. Our personal favorite at Ponce City Market is Honeysuckle Gelato with one of the best desserts in the whole city that'll definitely satisfy your sweeth tooth! The buildings main attraction is definitely the rooftop, an iconic destination in Atlanta that is pictured above. The rooftop has an 18-hole minigolf course, boardwalk style games, and an amazing view of the city. We could go on and on about Ponce City Market but we'll leave it for you and your family to explore.

2 / 5

#4: Callaway Gardens, Pine Mountain, GA

Next up on our list, at number four, is Callaway Gardens in Pine Mountain. Callaway Gardens is one of our favorite destinations because of its sheer beauty, all year long. From the sea of vibrant azalea blossoms in the spring to the Fantasy in Lights display in the winter, which is shown above. However, beyond its beauty, Callaway Gardens has a ton of things for you and your family to enjoy on its 2,500 acre plot of land, such as their variety of hiking/biking trails, their world-class golf course, or water sports on Robin Lake which boasts the world's largest man-made white sand beach. Callaway Gardens also features the Cecil B. Day Butterfly Center where over a thousand tropical butterflies flutter around. If you and your family are looking for an extended stay, Callaway Gardens also has a beautiful resort where you can enjoy everything Callaway Gardens has to offer in its entirety or lounge around and relax in the spa.

Explore Callaway Gardens more here!

3 / 5

#3: Lake Lanier, Gainesville, GA

At number three, we have Lake Lanier in Gainesville, Georgia. The things you and your family can do at Lake Lanier are endless. Lake Lanier is a hot spot in Georgia for its nearly 700 miles of shoreline where there are resorts, restaurants, beaches, parks, and more. Two resorts at Lake Lanier are Margaritaville and Lanier Islands, and both provide many activities for guests to enjoy such as their water parks, gaming areas, and lounges. Lake Lanier also offers rentals for boats, jet skis, kayaks, and canoes. At Lake Lanier you and your family can get out on the water to go tubing, wake boarding, or indulge yourselves in the many dining options, parks, or other outdoor and sport acitvities that Lake Lanier offers. Even during the winter the resorts offer snow tubing, ice skating, and carnival rides, making it a great destination for you and your family all year long.

Explore Lake Lanier more here!

4 / 5

#2: Savannah, GA

For our number two spot we had to put the entire city of Savannah. Savannah is captivating with its art, architecture, history, and location. Savannah is located on the shore of southeast Georgia, 20 minutes from Savannah's beach, Tybee Island. Savannah has plenty of things for you and your family to enjoy like shopping, indulging in the best restaurants southeast Georgia has to offer, and even spooky ghost tours. Savannah takes you on a journey back in time with its architecture showcasing how far back Savannah's history goes. At Savannah's waterfront you can find many restaurants, stores, candy stores, and more as you walk down River Street's enthralling cobblestone path. Savannah has so much to offer for you and your family to see and do all year round with plenty of festivals, events, and everything we mentioned before.

Explore the city of Savannah more here!

5 / 5

#1: Centennial Park District, Atlanta, GA

We'll be going back to Atlanta for our number one destination, Centennial Park District. In the heart of Atlanta, Centennial Park District is in the middle of just about everything you could possibly wanna do, from taking a tour through the World of Coca-Cola, the Gorgia Aquarium, Zoo Atlanta, or the many museums nearby. Centennial Olympic Park is also the perfect spot for you and your family to picnic and enjoy some time outdoors. Centennial Park also gives you a great view of the city with SkyView Atlanta, a 20 story ferris wheel towering over Centennial Olympic Park. With Centennial Park District being in the heart of the city, the dining, lodging, and shopping options are endless, making it the best place to be to really explore the city. There are many events, festivals, and activities throughout the year, providing you and your family with an amazing experience no matter what time of year you visit. We would never stop talking if we kept going because there's so much Centennial Park District has to offer, so we'll leave it for you and your family to explore!
