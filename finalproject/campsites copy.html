Our Top 5

Our goal at Visit GA is to point you in the right direction when planning your stay at one of Georgia's many sites and locations. This is why we've put together ourlist of the top five campsites for you and your family, throughout the Peach State.




1 / 5

#5: Chattahoochee Bend State Park, Newnan, GA

To kick off our list of the best campsites in Georgia, we'll start with our number five pick, Chattahoochee Bend State Park. One of Georgia's largest state parks at 2,910 acres, Chattahoochee Bend protects five miles of the Chattahoochee River with plenty of boat ramps for those who want to go boating, kayaking, or paddling, and has six miles of wooded trails for those wanting to hike or even take pictures. The lodging options here are almost endless with well over 50 different campsites. RV owners can park in one of the many RV campsites where they'll have elctricity, water, and waste hookups. For tent campers, there is the option of a riverfront site, walk-in sites, and traditional campsites. There are also bathhouses for campers to get a hot shower within walking distance of most campsites. There are also cottages and screened lodges available for families and groups to rent out. There are also a couple covered picnic shelters available for reservation and even a little playground for the kids.

2 / 5

#4: Mistletoe State Park, Appling, GA

Following Chattahoochee Bend State Park is our number four pick, Mistletoe State Park with it's lakefront location off of the 72,000 acre Clark Lake. Clark Lake is known for being one of the best bass fishing spots in the country with plenty of boatramps for easy access. Mistletoe State Park boasts a scenic shoreline, winding creeks, and miles of trails for guests to explore. The park is plesently placed on a peninsula where guests can witness the beautiful sunsets and sunrises. On the lake there are a mix of cottages and log cabins available for rent by groups and families, as well as a tent cabin with electricity and water. On top of that, there are over 100 tent, trailer, and RV campsites for all campers alike. There are also plenty of activities for guests to do like biking, birding, boating, fishing, hiking, swimming, and more!

Explore Mistletoe State Park more here!

3 / 5

#3: Skidaway Island State Park, Savannah, GA

Going into our top three, Skidaway Island State Park takes the number three spot. Skidaway Island State Park borders the Skidaway narrows of Georgia's Intracoastal Waterway and isn't far from the historic downtown Savannah. Skidaway Island is very scenic with it's trails going through the forests and marshes on the shore, where you'll find a boardwalk and an observation tower to enjoy the beautiful sunsets the park has to offer or watch the different wildlife running about. With over 100 campsites, shelters and campgrounds, guests have a wide variety of lodging to choose from to enjoy their stay at Skidaway Island State Park. With plenty of lodging options, and activities to choose from and its location near the city of Savannah and the beaches at Tybee Island, Skidaway Island is a great choice for you and your family to enjoy your stay here in the Peach State.

4 / 5

#2: Timberline Glamping Company

Taking a look at a different style of camping, Timberline Glamping Company takes our number two spot with its spin on camping, "glamping". Glamping is "glamorous camping" and offers more luxurious accommodations and facilities than traditional camping typically does. Timberline Glamping Company has many different locations all over northeastern Georgia, such as Lake Lanier, Amicalola Falls, Lake Blackshear, Unicoi State Park, and more! With Timberline Glamping Company guests have many different accomodations to choose from, like tents with one king bed, one queen bed, or even two queen beds. Most tents they offer have their own Keurig coffe maker, mini fridge, ceiling fans, and residential heating and air conditioning. On top of all that, every "glampsite" comes with hammocks, a wood fire ring, a charcoal grill, camp chairs, and picnic tables! Even though Timberline Glamping Co. differs from traditional camping, we felt it necessary to give them the second spot on this list, as this combines camping and luxury in the perfect fashion.

5 / 5

#1: Vogel State Park, Blairsville, GA

Finally, with the number one spot on our list, is Vogel State Park located in Blairsville. Vogel State Park is located within the Chattahoochee National Forest right at the base of Blood Mountain. This is also not too far from the highest point in Georgia, Brasstown Bald. With just under 150 cottages, shelters, and campsites, there is no lack of lodging for campers to choose from during their stay. There are also plenty of things for guests to do, such as fishing, hiking the 17 miles of trails, playing minigolf, and swimming, kayaking, or paddling on the 22 acre lake Vogel State Park has to offer. Vogel State Park also boasts an awe-inspiring waterfall, Trahylta Falls. On top of this, during the fall guests will be able to see the Blue Ridge Mountains painted with red, orange, and yellow leaves as seen above. There is also a rich history here as it is Georgia's second oldest state park, with many of the facilities were built during the Great Depression by the Civilian Conservation Corps. With its rich history and jaw-dropping scenery, Vogel State Park is one of the most beautiful locations within Georgia, which is why it easily takes our number one spot on the list.
